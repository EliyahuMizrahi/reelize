The Insight

Retention is structural, not stylistic.

What if you could borrow the exact retention structure of a video that already works — and use it to explain something hard?
Hooks, beat drops, scene rhythm, voice cadence — once you capture them, the "vibe" follows. Reelize reverse-engineers a Short you love and generates a brand-new video on that scaffold about any topic you choose.

Challenges

What broke — and how we fixed it.

The 48-hour story in four beats. Each was a near-project-killer.
Problem
Seven services, seven data shapes.
Demucs, Whisper, Shazam, librosa, pyannote, Gemini, ElevenLabs — each outputs data in its own shape and timing space. Half of the 48 hours went into the glue.
Solution
A single shared manifest every stage reads from and writes into. The models were commodities — orchestration was the product.
Problem
GPU contention melted memory.
Running Demucs + Whisper + pyannote + Gemini concurrently on one machine caused instant OOM under any real load.
Solution
Serialized job queue. Every request becomes a Postgres jobs row; an asyncio semaphore gates workers so only one GPU-heavy job runs at a time. Jobs survive restarts.
Problem
Script wouldn't land on the beat.
Narration generated from the transcript alone ignored the reference video's emphasis pattern — the thing that makes Shorts feel tight.
Solution
Feed the beat grid and energy envelope directly into Gemini's prompt so script emphasis lands on the existing drops.
Problem
Render timing drifted by milliseconds.
Voice, music, and footage came from different clocks. Single-pass scene detection missed half-second cuts on fast-edited Shorts.
Solution
Normalize every timing source to one clock before Remotion composition. Multi-pass keyframe refinement with pixel-level diffs for scene boundaries.
